# Isuwa-Jeremiah-and-Professor-Mohammed-Abdullahi-2026-Project
Diversity-Driven Firefly Algorithm for Explainable Machine Learning-Based Crop Yield Prediction

Abstract
Accurate crop yield prediction is essential for agricultural planning and food security. However, traditional models struggle to capture complex nonlinear interactions among soil, climate, and management factors, and they often suffer from high-dimensional noisy data and poor adaptability to climate variability. To address these challenges, this study develops a machine learning-based crop yield prediction model that employs a hybrid Beta Distribution-Oppositional-Based Learning (OBL) Firefly Algorithm feature selection to identify key soil, climatic, and management features, predicts yield using a KNN Regressor, and leverages SHAP to interpret feature contributions and enhance model transparency. The approach is benchmarked against using all features, the standard Firefly Algorithm, and variants enhanced individually with Beta distribution or OBL. Results over 10 independent runs show that the proposed hybrid method achieves superior predictive performance with the lowest error (MAE = 1.0096, RMSE = 1.3002) and consistently selects fewer most informative features. SHAP analysis identifies ‘Crop Type’, ‘Fertilizer_Used’, and ‘Rainfall’as the primary drivers of yield, reflecting real-world agronomic knowledge. Overall, the proposed method improves prediction accuracy, stability, and interpretability, offering a reliable tool for informed crop management and decision-making.
